Dental Crowns

Restore strength and appearance to damaged teeth

A dental crown is a custom-made, tooth-shaped cap that is placed over a damaged or weakened tooth to restore its shape, size, strength, and appearance. RAM DENTAL CLINIC recommends crowns in various situations:

  • Protecting a weak tooth from breaking or holding together parts of a cracked tooth
  • Restoring a severely worn down or broken tooth
  • Covering and supporting a tooth with a large filling when not much natural tooth remains
  • Holding a dental bridge in place
  • Covering misshapen or severely discolored teeth
  • Covering a dental implant
  • Protecting a tooth that has had root canal treatment

Dental Bridges

Replace missing teeth with fixed prosthetics

A dental bridge is a fixed prosthetic device that literally "bridges" the gap created by one or more missing teeth. Bridges are anchored to the natural teeth or implants on either side of the gap, known as abutment teeth, and contain artificial teeth called pontics in between.

RAM DENTAL CLINIC may recommend a dental bridge to:

  • Restore your smile and facial shape
  • Improve your ability to properly chew and speak
  • Maintain the alignment of your remaining teeth
  • Distribute the forces in your bite properly by replacing missing teeth
  • Prevent remaining teeth from drifting out of position

Types of Bridges We Offer

Traditional Bridges

The most common type, with crowns on the abutment teeth supporting the pontic in between.

Cantilever Bridges

Used when there are adjacent teeth on only one side of the missing tooth.

Maryland Bridges

Conservative option using metal or porcelain wings bonded to adjacent teeth.

Implant-Supported Bridges

The most stable option, using dental implants instead of crowns or frameworks.

The Crown & Bridge Process

What to expect during your treatment

Initial Consultation

RAM DENTAL CLINIC will evaluate your dental health, discuss your needs and goals, and determine if crowns or bridges are appropriate for your situation. This may include X-rays and impressions to get a complete picture of your mouth.

Treatment Planning

Based on the examination, RAM DENTAL CLINIC will develop a customized treatment plan, discussing material options, procedures, timeline, and costs. For bridges, the design and number of pontics will be determined.

Tooth Preparation

The affected tooth or teeth are prepared by reshaping them to create space for the crown or bridge. For bridges, the abutment teeth are prepared to receive crowns. Local anesthesia ensures your comfort during this process.

Impressions & Temporary Restoration

Precise impressions are taken of the prepared teeth to create an exact model for fabricating your custom crown or bridge. A temporary crown or bridge is placed to protect your teeth while the permanent restoration is being crafted.

Laboratory Fabrication

Your impressions are sent to a dental laboratory where skilled technicians craft your custom crown or bridge according to Our specifications. This typically takes 1-2 weeks, depending on the complexity and materials used.

Final Placement

Once your permanent restoration is ready, the temporary is removed, and the new crown or bridge is carefully checked for fit, bite alignment, and aesthetics before being permanently cemented in place.

Follow-up Care

RAM DENTAL CLINIC will provide detailed instructions for caring for your new restoration and schedule a follow-up appointment to ensure everything is functioning properly and comfortably.

Caring for Your Crowns & Bridges

Tips for maintaining your restorations

Daily Care

  • Brush twice daily with a soft-bristled toothbrush
  • Floss daily, using floss threaders or special bridge flossers if necessary
  • Consider using an antibacterial mouthwash
  • Pay special attention to the gum line where the crown meets the natural tooth
  • For bridges, clean thoroughly under and around the pontic

Dietary Considerations

  • Avoid extremely hard foods that could damage your restorations
  • Be cautious with sticky foods that could potentially dislodge a crown or bridge
  • Minimize consumption of staining substances like coffee, tea, and tobacco
  • Chew on both sides of your mouth to distribute pressure evenly
  • Do not use your teeth as tools to open packages or bottles

Regular Check-ups

  • Maintain regular dental check-ups every six months
  • Professional cleanings help keep your restorations in good condition
  • Early detection of any issues can prevent more serious problems
  • RAM DENTAL CLINIC will monitor the condition of your crowns or bridges
  • The health of supporting teeth and gums will be assessed

When to Contact Us

Contact RAM DENTAL CLINIC's clinic immediately if you experience:

  • Pain or discomfort when biting down
  • A loose or dislodged crown or bridge
  • Chips, cracks, or damage to the restoration
  • Persistent sensitivity around the treated tooth
  • Changes in your bite alignment
