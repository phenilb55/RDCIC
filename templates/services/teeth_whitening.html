In-Office Professional Whitening

Immediate results in a single visit

Our in-office teeth whitening procedure is a quick and effective way to dramatically whiten your teeth in just one appointment. This treatment is perfect for patients who want immediate results or are preparing for a special event such as a wedding, job interview, or photoshoot.

The Procedure

  1. Preparation: We begin by examining your teeth and discussing your whitening goals. A thorough cleaning may be recommended before whitening to remove any surface stains and ensure optimal results.
  2. Protection: We carefully protect your gums and soft tissue with a special barrier, isolating just your teeth for treatment.

  5. Multiple Applications: The gel is typically applied in several 15-minute intervals, with the entire procedure taking about 60-90 minutes.
  6. Results: Many patients see their teeth brighten by several shades in just one session.

Our in-office whitening treatment is safe, comfortable, and provides immediate, dramatic results.

Understanding Tooth Discoloration

Common causes of stained or yellowed teeth

Before addressing tooth discoloration, it's helpful to understand what causes it. Tooth discoloration can be categorized into three main types:

Extrinsic Stains

These affect the outer layer (enamel) of your teeth and are typically caused by:

  • Coffee, tea, and red wine
  • Tobacco products
  • Colored foods and sauces
  • Some types of medications
  • Poor oral hygiene

Intrinsic Stains

These occur within the inner structure (dentin) of the tooth and may be caused by:

  • Excessive fluoride exposure in childhood
  • Tetracycline antibiotics during tooth development
  • Dental trauma
  • Certain genetic conditions
  • Aging

Age-Related

As we age, our teeth naturally become discolored due to:

  • Enamel thinning, revealing more of the yellower dentin
  • Cumulative effect of staining substances
  • Natural darkening of dentin over time
  • Wear and tear on tooth surfaces
  • Previous dental work

What to Expect

Important information about teeth whitening

Safety and Comfort

Professional teeth whitening at RAM DENTAL CLINIC is safe and comfortable. Some patients may experience temporary tooth sensitivity or gum irritation, but these effects are typically mild and subside quickly. Our dental team takes precautions to minimize these effects and ensure your comfort throughout the process.

Results and Longevity

While results vary based on the initial shade of your teeth and the cause of discoloration, most patients experience a significant improvement in tooth color. The longevity of your whitening results depends on your habits and oral hygiene. Avoiding staining substances and maintaining good oral care can help your results last from several months to a few years.

Candidates for Whitening

Most people are good candidates for teeth whitening, but it may not be suitable for everyone. Whitening works best on natural tooth enamel and may not be effective on dental restorations like crowns, veneers, or fillings. During your consultation, we'll assess your oral health and discuss whether whitening is right for you or if alternative cosmetic treatments would be more appropriate.

Maintenance and Touch-ups

To maintain your bright smile, we recommend periodic touch-up treatments. If you've chosen our take-home whitening option, you can use your custom trays for occasional touch-ups. We also suggest regular dental cleanings and good oral hygiene practices. Limiting consumption of staining food and beverages and avoiding tobacco products will help preserve your white smile.

Maintaining Your White Smile

Tips for prolonging your whitening results

Dietary Considerations

What you eat and drink can significantly impact the whiteness of your teeth. Consider these tips:

  • Limit consumption of staining foods and beverages like coffee, tea, red wine, and dark-colored sauces
  • When drinking staining beverages, use a straw to minimize contact with your teeth
  • Rinse your mouth with water after consuming staining substances
  • Include "detergent" foods like apples, carrots, and celery in your diet, which help clean teeth as you chew
  • Avoid tobacco products, which cause severe staining

Oral Hygiene Practices

Maintaining excellent oral hygiene is essential for preserving your whitening results:

  • Brush your teeth at least twice daily with a whitening toothpaste
  • Floss daily to remove plaque and debris between teeth
  • Consider using an electric toothbrush for more effective cleaning
  • Rinse with an anti-staining mouthwash
  • Schedule regular dental cleanings every six months

Maintenance Treatments

To keep your smile bright over the long term:

  • Use your custom whitening trays for occasional touch-ups as recommended
  • Ask about whitening maintenance protocols during your regular check-ups
  • Consider a professional whitening "boost" once a year
  • Use a whitening pen for quick touch-ups when needed
  • Follow your dentist's personalized recommendations for your specific situation

Protecting Your Investment

Additional measures to safeguard your white smile:

  • Wear a mouthguard during sports to prevent trauma to teeth
  • If you grind your teeth, use a night guard to prevent wear on enamel
  • Address any dental issues promptly to maintain overall oral health
  • Stay hydrated to maintain saliva flow, which naturally cleanses teeth
  • Consider cosmetic bonding or veneers for teeth that don't respond well to whitening
