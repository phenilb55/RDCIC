Full Mouth Rehabilitation

Comprehensive restoration of dental function and aesthetics

Full mouth rehabilitation at RAM DENTAL CLINIC's dental clinic is a comprehensive approach to restoring optimal dental health, function, and appearance for patients with extensive dental problems. This transformative treatment addresses multiple issues simultaneously, providing a complete solution for even the most complex cases.

Whether your dental problems are the result of injury, decay, wear, or congenital conditions, our tailored full mouth rehabilitation can restore your smile's health, comfort, and beauty.

When Full Mouth Rehabilitation Is Recommended

Complex dental issues that benefit from a comprehensive approach

Multiple Missing Teeth

When several teeth are missing throughout the mouth, affecting both appearance and the ability to chew properly.

Extensive Tooth Wear

Severe wear from grinding (bruxism), acid erosion, or aging that has significantly reduced tooth height or structure.

TMJ Disorders

Chronic jaw pain, clicking, or restricted movement related to temporomandibular joint dysfunction and bite issues.

Advanced Decay or Damage

Multiple teeth affected by severe decay, fractures, or old restorations that are failing throughout the mouth.

Bite Discrepancies

Improper bite alignment causing functional problems, accelerated wear, or difficulty chewing effectively.

Aesthetic Concerns

Comprehensive aesthetic improvements desired for multiple teeth including color, shape, size, and alignment issues.

The Full Mouth Rehabilitation Process

Your journey to a completely restored smile

Comprehensive Evaluation

RAM DENTAL CLINIC performs a thorough examination of your teeth, gums, jaw joints, muscles, and bite. This includes digital X-rays, photographs, and impressions to create accurate models of your mouth.

Diagnosis and Treatment Planning

Based on the detailed evaluation, RAM DENTAL CLINIC develops a customized treatment plan addressing all aspects of your oral health, function, and aesthetics in the optimal sequence.

Preliminary Treatments

Any urgent issues are addressed first, including treatment of active disease, extractions of hopeless teeth, and management of pain or infection.

Establishing Proper Bite

Correcting bite issues is often a critical early step, which may involve orthodontics, equilibration (selective reshaping), or temporary restorations to establish the ideal jaw position.

Restorative Phase

Implementing the planned restorative procedures such as dental implants, crowns, bridges, veneers, or dentures to rebuild and restore teeth to proper form and function.

Final Aesthetic Refinements

Fine-tuning the appearance of your restored smile with procedures such as teeth whitening, minor shape adjustments, or gum contouring as needed.

Long-term Maintenance

Development of a customized maintenance plan including regular check-ups, professional cleanings, and potentially night guards or other protective devices to ensure the longevity of your rehabilitation.

Treatment Options in Full Mouth Rehabilitation

Customized solutions for your unique needs

Dental Implants

Permanent titanium root replacements that support crowns, bridges, or dentures, restoring both function and appearance for missing teeth.

Crowns & Bridges

Custom-made porcelain restorations that repair severely damaged teeth or replace missing teeth by anchoring to adjacent healthy teeth.

Dentures

Traditional removable prosthetics or modern implant-supported versions that replace multiple teeth or full arches with natural-looking results.

Root Canal Therapy

Treatment that preserves severely infected or damaged teeth by removing diseased inner pulp and sealing the chamber to prevent further infection.

Periodontal Treatment

Professional cleanings, scaling and root planing, or surgical interventions to address gum disease and create a healthy foundation for restorations.

Orthodontics

Traditional braces or clear aligners to correct alignment issues, creating proper bite function and improved aesthetics.
